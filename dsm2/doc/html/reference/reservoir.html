Reservoirs

Overview:

Reservoirs are open bodies of water that store flow and are connected to nodes by means of an energy-based equation. Reservoirs are considered instantly well-mixed. The Reservoirs Table specifies the identity and physical properties of the reservoir. Connections to nodes are specified in the Reservoir Connections table.

Reservoir Table

The Reservoir table defines the name and physical properties of the reservoir. The Reservoirs table is a top-level layered table.

Field Descriptions

Layer
Layer number on which the entry is defined.
Name
Name of the reservoir. This is the identifier of the reservoir used in other GUI views.
Area (million sq ft)
Surface area of the reservoir at typical depth. This area is used to calculate volume changes.
Bottom Elev (ft)
Elevation of the bottom of the reservoir.
Use
Check box indicating item is in use. See Use Column.

Reservoir Connections Table

This table lists reservoir connections to neighboring nodes. Flow through reservoir connections is calculated using the following formula

Q = Cto sqrt[ 2g(znode - zres) ] ... zres < znode

Q = Cfrom sqrt[ 2g(zres - znode) ] ... zres > znode

Where:

  • Cto and Cfrom are coefficients representing the hydraulic efficiency of the reservoir connection and the nominal Area perpendicular to flow.
  • g is gravity and
  • zres and znode are the water surface elevations at the reservoir and node (node surface is assessed by means of a reference channel that has no reservoirs attached to it).

Field Descriptions

Node
Number identifying the node at which connection is specified.
Res Coef (out)
Coefficient to node, greater than zero. If you compare the reservoir equation to the gate or other orifice equation you will find that the reservoir coefficient actually folds several quantities into one parameter: a flow efficiency (between zero and one) and a area of flow. If you have an observation of the area normal to flow, the coefficient should be some fraction of this aperture.
Res Coef (in)
Same as coefficient to node, but for a flow direction out of the reservoir.

Usage Notes:

  • A node may not have more than three reservoir connections **** and must have at least one ungated channel connection.
